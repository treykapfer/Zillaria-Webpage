Soldura

Those who venture past the dunes of Byaste may reach the shadowlands of the east. According to legends of the leonin and humans who reside in the area, a wrathful god of judgement watches them from above. On clearer days, when the eternal storm is not so violent, locals say they can see the shadow of the two headed, winged beast through the rays of the suns. It is said the creature has three mouths, and eyes upon its tail, which gaze and judge the remnants of the Alden Reich who settled the area. Soldura is worshipped as a God of divine judgement, who led the charge against the fallen twins when they ravaged the lands of Zillaria. The floating steps are thought to be created by the titan aura of the resting God, as it slumbers atop the clouds.

Balcoth

Legend says that in the Ring of Balcoth, beneath the molten rock, lies an ancient demonic power - one of shadow and flame. Those who wander in the caverns of the ring claim to have visions of a black beast with great ram horns, bound in chains. Goblins, cambions, and minotaurs worship this terrifying titan as a vengeful God. Shamans and other magic practitioners in the local area around the ring draw upon the ancient power of Balcoth and wield it for their own gain. Balcoth is an inspiring figure for the enslaved Goblin race, who like Balcoth, hope to one day break free of their chains. It is rumored that cambions were once elves who were corrupted by Balcoth’s power long ago. Prophets say that Balcoth was chained to the mountain after causing a great catastophy in the time before humans defiled Zillaria. Rumor has it that if one can reach Balcoth and defeat him, they can pass through the terrasphere into Col.
